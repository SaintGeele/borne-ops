How to Set Up a VPN on Your Work Device
By Geele Evans

In these instructions I am going to show you how to set up a Virtual Private Network (VPN) on your work computer. A VPN creates a secure, encrypted connection between your device and the internet, protecting your data from hackers on public networks and keeping your company's information safe while you work from home. To do this, I will show you how to choose a VPN service, download the app, and connect to a secure server. Note: Your employer may provide a specific VPN service—if so, skip to Step 3 and use the service they recommend.

Choosing a VPN Service
1. Research reputable VPN providers. Look for services with a no-log policy (they do not track your browsing history), strong encryption (AES-256 bit), and good customer reviews. Popular options include NordVPN, ExpressVPN, and Proton VPN.
2. Check if your employer offers a corporate VPN. If they do, they will provide credentials and specific setup instructions—use those instead of a personal VPN.
3. Sign up for an account. Most VPNs offer a monthly or yearly subscription. Choose a plan that fits your budget and provides the features you need.

Downloading the VPN App
1. Go to the official website of your chosen VPN provider. Avoid third-party download sites to ensure you get the legitimate software.
2. Click on the "Download" button and select the version compatible with your operating system (Windows, macOS, Linux, etc.).
3. Once the installer file downloads, open it by double-clicking the file in your Downloads folder.
4. Follow the on-screen prompts to install the application. This typically involves clicking "Next," "Agree," and entering your admin password when prompted.

Logging In and Connecting
1. Open the VPN application. You can do this by clicking the icon on your desktop or searching for the app in your Start menu (Windows) or Applications folder (Mac).
2. Log in using the credentials you created when you signed up for the VPN service.
3. Select a server to connect to. For better speed, choose a server closest to your physical location. If your employer requires you to connect to a specific region or server for work purposes, select that one instead.
4. Click the "Connect" button. The app will establish a secure connection. Once connected, you will typically see a confirmation message or a green indicator.

Verifying Your Connection
1. Check that the VPN is active. Most apps display a "Connected" status and may show your new IP address.
2. Visit a website like whatismyip.com to confirm your IP address has changed to match the VPN server location you selected.
3. For added security, enable the "Kill Switch" feature in your VPN settings. This automatically disconnects your internet if the VPN connection drops, preventing data leaks.

Disconnecting When Finished
1. When you are done working, open the VPN application.
2. Click the "Disconnect" button to end the secure connection.
3. Close the application to ensure no background connections remain active.

Tips for Ongoing Use
- Keep your VPN app updated to receive the latest security patches.
- Reconnect to the VPN every time you start a work session, especially when using public Wi-Fi at coffee shops or airports.
- If you experience slow speeds, try connecting to a different server location closer to your actual location.
