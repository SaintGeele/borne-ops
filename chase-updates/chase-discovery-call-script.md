# Chase Discovery Call Script — 30 Minutes
**Purpose: Qualify, not close. Schedule the audit.**
**Tagline: "We don't just install AI. We stay."**

---

## Before the Call

1. Review lead source and any notes from pre-call research
2. Know their industry (dental, HVAC, plumbing, contractor)
3. Know their company size (1-10, 11-50 employees)
4. Have their location (CT, NY, NJ, MA) noted
5. Pull up your qualifying rubric

**Your goal entering the call:** Find out if they have a real AI problem worth auditing. If yes, sell the audit. If no, add to nurture list. Do not sell products.

---

## Call Structure

### [0:00–2:00] Opening + Agenda

"Thanks for making time today, [Name]. Before we jump in, here's what I want to do: I have some questions about your business, and you have some questions about us. By the end of the call, you should have a clear picture of where you stand with AI — whether you work with us or not. Sound good?"

*[Wait for confirmation. If they want to jump straight to pricing or a demo, redirect:]*

"I'd love to get you a demo, but I find demos are only useful if they're relevant to your specific situation. So I'd rather spend 25 minutes understanding your business first, and then I can tell you honestly whether we're a fit. Fair?"

---

### [2:00–7:00] Understand Their Business

"I'd love to start with you. Tell me a bit about [practice / company] — what do you all do, how big is the team, and what's the main thing you're trying to grow right now?"

*[Listen actively. Mirror back what you hear.]*

"I hear you. So [summarize what they said: e.g., 'you've been in business for X years, you're doing about Y in revenue, you're trying to get more new patients per month' — does that sound right?']"

**Probing questions:**
- "How many people are on your team right now?"
- "What's the biggest growth challenge you're facing this quarter?"
- "Are you working on anything specific around [their stated goal]?"

---

### [7:00–14:00] Map the Current Customer Acquisition Flow

"I want to understand how new customers come into your business right now. Walk me through what happens when someone hears about you for the first time — all the way to when they become a customer."

*[Let them walk through it. Don't interrupt. Take notes.]*

**Follow-up questions based on what they share:**

*If they mention phone calls:*
- "What happens when someone calls in? Who's handling that right now?"
- "Do you have a sense of how many calls you're getting, and how many you're missing?"

*If they mention forms or web leads:*
- "How fast does someone follow up when someone fills out a form?"
- "Is that follow-up automated, or is it manual?"

*If they mention referrals:*
- "Where are most of your referrals coming from right now?"
- "Do you have a system for following up with referral sources?"

*If they mention Google / ads:*
- "What happens after someone clicks on your ad — where do they go?"
- "Is the handoff from online to in-person seamless?"

---

### [14:00–22:00] Find the Pain Points

"You've walked me through the flow — now I want to dig into where it might be leaking. When you think about your business right now, what's the part that frustrates you the most — the place where deals go cold or leads slip through?"

*[Let them identify the pain. Push gently to get specific.]*

**Qualifying question (CRITICAL):**
"On a scale of 1 to 10 — with 10 being 'this is costing me serious money and I need to fix it yesterday' and 1 being 'it's annoying but not urgent' — how frustrated are you with [their specific pain point]?"

*[If 7 or above: strong candidate. If 6 or below: nurture, not ready for audit.]*

**Follow-up on the score:**
"What makes it a [their number] instead of a lower number?"

*[Get the revenue implication if possible:]*

"Can you put a number on what that's costing you? Even roughly — per month or per quarter?"

---

### [22:00–26:00] Introduce the Audit (Don't Pitch)

"Based on what you've shared, [their pain] is a real problem — and it sounds like it's affecting your [revenue / capacity / growth]. The reason I ask about this stuff is that we work differently than most AI vendors."

"We don't just show up, install a tool, and leave. We start with a structured audit — we spend two weeks mapping your business, interviewing your team, identifying exactly where AI can have the biggest impact, and giving you a roadmap with real numbers."

"The reason we do it that way is simple: we don't want to sell you something that doesn't fit. The audit tells us both whether there's a real opportunity here."

---

### [26:00–30:00] Close for the Audit

"We offer two versions depending on where you are:"

"For businesses at your stage, I'd actually recommend [option — smaller/lower cost for smaller businesses]."

"[Description of recommended option] — starts at $[price]. We'd complete it within [timeframe], and you'd walk away with a complete picture of your AI opportunities, specific ROI numbers, and a 30/90/180 day roadmap."

"If that makes sense, the next step would be to get a few key dates on the calendar and send you a prep sheet. If the timing isn't right, I'd rather you tell me that now than force something — we can stay in touch and revisit when it makes more sense."

"So — is that something you want to move forward with, or should we keep the conversation open for a bit?"

---

## If They Say Yes — Next Steps

1. "Great — let me send over a quick proposal with the scope and timeline, and we'll get some time on the calendar."
2. Confirm their email
3. Tell them what to expect: prep sheet within 24 hours, kickoff call scheduled
4. Set expectations: "The audit itself takes about two weeks — we'll be doing stakeholder interviews and reviewing your current flows. You don't have to do much beyond being available for a couple of calls."

## If They Push Back / Need to Think

"Don't feel pressured — this is a real investment, and I want you to be confident about it. What I'd suggest is this: take the next [week / few days] and think it through. If you want to run it by anyone else on your team, that makes sense."

"If you decide to move forward, reach out and mention my name. If you decide it's not the right fit, no hard feelings — I might reach back out in a few months to see how things are going."

"Can I send you a link to our [Audit Readiness Checklist / free quiz] in the meantime? It's five minutes and it'll give you some useful framework even if you never work with us."

## If They Try to Skip to a Demo

"I hear you — you want to see the product. I get it. But here's my concern: if I show you our AI receptionist and you don't have the problem it's designed to solve, it'll just be noise. I want to show you something that's actually relevant to [their pain]."

"Let me ask you this: if I could show you exactly how much money [their pain] is costing you every month — would that be worth 30 minutes of your time?"

*[If yes → proceed to qualifying question.]*

*[If no → "No problem at all. Want me to send over some materials you can review on your own? We can talk when you're ready."]*

---

## Qualifying Rubric

**Strong candidate (book the audit):**
- Pain score 7+
- Identifiable revenue impact
- Budget authority or access to decision-maker
- Timeline: within 90 days
- Already using some AI (has something to audit)

**Weak candidate (nurture):**
- Pain score below 6
- No quantifiable revenue impact
- No budget authority
- "Not looking to change anything right now"
- No existing AI (no baseline to compare)

---

## Key Rules for Chase on This Call

1. **You are a diagnostician, not a salesperson.** You're finding problems. You're not selling tools.
2. **Never demo the product on this call.** Demo = you're a vendor. No demo = you're a consultant.
3. **The qualifying question is non-negotiable.** If they won't give you a pain score, they're not serious.
4. **"We don't just install AI. We stay."** — Use this when describing what makes Borne different. Not as a tagline. As a practice.
5. **The audit is the product. The product comes after.** You are selling the audit. Implementation is a future conversation.
6. **Be ready to take no for an answer.** If they're not ready, nurture is better than push.
7. **Always leave with value.** Even if they don't buy, they should leave with a clearer picture of their AI situation.
