How to Set Up a VPN on Your Work Device
By Geele Evans

In this guide, I'm going to walk you through setting up a VPN on your work computer. A VPN (Virtual Private Network) encrypts your internet connection so hackers on public networks can't steal your data, and it keeps your company's information safe when you're working from home. I'll show you how to pick a VPN, download the app, and connect to a secure server.

Note: If your employer already gives you a VPN to use, skip ahead to Step 3 and just follow their instructions instead.

Choosing a VPN Service
1. Do some quick research on VPN providers. Look for a "no log" policy (they don't track what you browse), strong encryption (256 bit), and good reviews. NordVPN, ExpressVPN, and Proton VPN are solid options.
2. Check if your company offers a corporate VPN. If they do, use theirs. They'll give you login credentials and setup steps.
3. Sign up for an account. Pick a monthly or yearly plan that fits your budget.

Downloading the VPN App
1. Head to your VPN provider's official website. Don't download from random third party sites, make sure it's the real deal.
2. Click "Download" and choose the version for your operating system (Windows, macOS, Linux, etc.).
3. Find the installer in your Downloads folder and double click it.
4. Click through the prompts, enter your admin password when asked.

Logging In and Connecting
1. Open the VPN app. You can find it in your Applications folder (Mac) or Start menu (Windows).
2. Log in with the account you created in Step 1.
3. Pick a server to connect to. For the best speed, choose one closest to you. If your job requires a specific region, select that one instead.
4. Hit "Connect." Once it connects, you'll see a confirmation message or green indicator.

Verifying Your Connection
1. Check that the VPN shows as "Connected" in the app.
2. Go to whatismyip.com to confirm your IP address changed to match the server you picked.
3. Turn on the "Kill Switch" in your VPN settings. This cuts your internet if the VPN drops, so your data doesn't leak.

Disconnecting When Finished
1. When you're done working, open the VPN app.
2. Click "Disconnect" to end the secure connection.
3. Close the app completely to make sure no connections are running in the background.

Tips for Ongoing Use
1. Keep your VPN app updated. New versions patch security holes.
2. Connect every time you work, especially on public Wi-Fi at coffee shops or airports.
3. If the connection feels slow, try a different server location closer to you.
