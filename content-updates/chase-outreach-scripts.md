## OBJECTION HANDLERS

### "We already have AI / we're working with someone."
"That's great — most businesses we talk to do. The question isn't whether you have AI. It's whether your AI is actually performing at the level it was when it was first installed. Most of our clients have AI — they just don't know if it's working anymore. That's exactly what our audit measures."

### "We're not looking to change anything right now."
"Totally understand. The audit isn't about changing — it's about knowing. You might already have everything optimized. But most businesses we look at find at least one area where AI quietly stopped working the way it should. Understanding your baseline costs you nothing. Want me to send over the quiz so you can see where you stand?"

### "We don't have budget for an audit right now."
"No problem. Our self-service audit is $497 — that's five questions, a 30-minute debrief, and a written report on your top opportunities. It's designed to be accessible. If that's not the right fit, stay on our radar — AI maintenance becomes a bigger problem the longer it goes unaddressed."

### "This sounds like a sales pitch."
"It's not — I promise. The discovery call is us asking questions about your business and you telling us where the gaps are. If we find a real opportunity and it makes sense, we'll talk about next steps. If we don't, we'll tell you that too. Either way, you'll leave with a clearer picture of where you stand."

## DISCOVERY CALL SCRIPT (30 MIN — QUALIFY, NOT CLOSE)

**Goal:** Schedule an audit. Do not demo products. Do not close.

---

### Minute 0–5: Rapport + Understand Their Business
"Thanks for making time today. Before I walk you through anything, I want to understand your business a bit. Tell me what you all do — your practice, your team, what you're focused on this year."

*[Listen. Don't pitch. Mirror back what they say.]*

---

### Minute 5–15: Current Customer Acquisition Flow
"I'd love to understand how you currently bring in customers. Walk me through what happens when a new lead comes in — from first touch to when they become a customer."

*Probing questions:*
- "Where do most of your leads come from right now?"
- "What happens after someone fills out a form or calls in?"
- "Is there a gap between when someone expresses interest and when someone actually follows up?"

---

### Minute 15–25: Pain Points + Revenue Leaks
"Where do you feel like you're losing the most — either leads that go cold, deals that don't close, or customers that don't come back?"

*[Let them talk. Find the pain that matters most.]*

- "On a scale of 1–10, how frustrated are you with how your business handles [their specific pain]?"
- "How long has that been a problem?"
- "Have you tried anything to fix it?"

---

### Minute 25–28: Planting the Audit Seed
"If I could show you exactly where AI would have the biggest impact on your business — with specific numbers and a clear roadmap — would that be something you'd want to see?"

*[Pause. Let them respond.]*

---

### Minute 28–30: Close for Audit
"Here's what I'd recommend based on what you've shared. The right next step is a proper AI audit. We'd spend two weeks going deep — interviews with your team, mapping your processes, building a roadmap with specific ROI projections."

"We offer two versions: a self-service audit at $497 for smaller teams, or our full two-week audit at $5,000 for businesses at your scale."

"Based on what you've described, I'd actually recommend starting with [option]. Would you want to move forward with that — or should we stay in touch and revisit this when the timing's better?"

---

## KEY FRAMING REMINDERS FOR CHASE

1. **Never pitch a product on the first call.** Pitch the audit. The product comes after.
2. **You are a diagnostician, not a salesperson.** You find problems. You don't sell tools.
3. **"We don't just install AI. We stay."** — This is the differentiator. Lean on it.
4. **Qualifying question:** "On a scale of 1–10, how frustrated are you with [specific pain]?" — If they're below a 6, they're not ready. Nurture.
5. **The audit is not the close.** It's the entry point. The close comes after.
