# Borne Systems — Discovery Call Script
## For: Carson Aesthetics, MA Home Improvement, Apex Home Improvement (Week 1 Warm Leads)

---

## BEFORE THE CALL

**Prep:**
- Pull their lead info from Notion (company, industry, notes from previous conversations)
- Know their average job value if available
- Know how many leads they're getting per week if available
- Have your calendar open to book the $497 audit or 15-min readiness call

**Goal of the call:**
- Diagnose, not sell
- Ask questions until THEY identify the problem
- If there's a fit, offer the $497 audit as the logical next step
- If no fit, thank them and move on

---

## SCRIPT

### OPENING (0:00–0:30)

> "Hey [Name], thanks for making time. I really appreciate it. Before we start — I'm not here to pitch you anything or try to sell you something you don't need. I just want to understand how your business runs, and if there's a way I might be able to help, I'll tell you straight. Fair?
>
> Can you walk me through what a typical [day/week] looks like for your team?"

**Purpose:** Put them at ease. Remove the "I'm being sold to" feeling. Get them talking.

---

### DIAGNOSIS QUESTIONS (0:30–12:00)

Ask one at a time. Let them answer fully before moving on. Take notes.

**Question 1 — Time traps:**
> "What are the things you spend the most time on that don't directly bring in revenue? Like paperwork, scheduling, follow-ups — the stuff that's necessary but takes you away from what you actually got paid to do?"

**Question 2 — Lead capture:**
> "How many calls or inquiries do you get in an average week? And out of those, how many would you say you actually talk to — versus missed calls, voicemails that slip through, or inquiries you didn't get back to in time?"

*(Follow-up if they mention missed calls:)*
> "What do you think that costs you in lost jobs? Rough estimate is fine."

**Question 3 — After-hours:**
> "What happens when someone calls you after you're closed? Or on the weekend. Is that a black hole or does something catch it?"

**Question 4 — Current AI/tools:**
> "Do you have any automation or AI tools in place right now? Like a chatbot, an answering service, anything like that? How's that working for you?"

*(If they say no:)*
> "Got it — so every call that comes in is either answered or missed. Nothing in between."

*(If they say yes:)*
> "What do you like about it? What do you wish it did better?"

**Question 5 — The one thing:**
> "If you could wave a magic wand and fix one thing in your business tomorrow — not the biggest, just the one thing that's been nagging at you — what would it be?"

---

### BRIDGE TO RECOMMENDATION (12:00–14:00)

*(Based on what they said, mirror back the problem:)*

> "Okay, so here's what I'm hearing: [repeat their biggest problem in 1-2 sentences]. Is that fair?"

*(Then:)*

> "What we've found is that most businesses in your situation have a similar issue — they're leaving money on the table because they don't have a system that actually captures every opportunity and follows up automatically. The thing is, most AI tools they buy either don't work the way they expected, or they work for a month and then nobody maintains them and they drift."

> "That's the problem we built Borne Systems to solve. We don't just install AI. We stay — we make sure it actually keeps working."

---

### RECOMMENDATION (14:00–15:00)

*(Don't say "I want to sell you" — frame it as their decision:)*

> "Here's where I'd start if I were you: Our $497 AI Audit is a 30-minute call where we go through your whole operation and I put together a written report that shows you exactly where AI is costing you money — and exactly how to fix it. We also give you ROI estimates for each recommendation before you spend a dollar."

> "It's not for everyone. But if the things you're telling me are real problems you want to solve — I'd recommend starting there. No obligation, you get a real document you can use. What do you think?"

**If they say yes:**
> "Let me pull up my calendar. We can get this on the schedule this week or next — whatever works better for you."

**If they hesitate:**
> "No pressure at all. I want to make sure this makes sense for you. What's holding you back — is it the price, the time, or are you just not sure if it's a priority right now?"

*(Handle the objection — see below)*

**If they say no or not now:**
> "Got it, completely understand. Can I check back in [next month/quarter]? Business changes and this might make more sense down the road."

---

## OBJECTION HANDLERS

### "I don't have time for a call."

> "That's actually exactly why this makes sense — the call is 30 minutes and it might save you 5, 10 hours a week once we fix what's broken. What are you doing Tuesday or Wednesday — I've got a slot open."

### "It's too expensive."

> "I hear you. Before you rule it out — what's your average job worth to you? [Let them answer.] And how many missed calls do you think you have in a month? [Let them answer.] If we could show you that fixing this would pay for itself in [X weeks/months], would that change how you see the price?"

### "I already have someone handling this."

> "That's great — honestly. The question is whether they're tracking results. Most AI implementations we see aren't being monitored. Want to find out if what you have is actually working at full capacity?"

### "I need to think about it."

> "Of course, no rush. When would be a better time — end of the month, next quarter? I'll put a reminder to check in. What would need to be true for you to want to move forward at that point?"

## REMEMBER

- You're not selling. You're diagnosing.
- Silence is okay. Let them talk.
- If there's no real problem, they won't be a good client anyway.
- Your confidence comes from knowing the product works — trust that.
- The worst thing that happens is they say no. That's fine.
